Working Papers

  1. Working Paper
    Regulatory Complexity and Corporate Venture Capital
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abstract SSRN Link

    I examine how regulatory fragmentation affects corporate venture capital (CVC) investment. Using firm-level measures of regulatory fragmentation for active CVC investors, I document a robust non-monotonic relationship: moderate fragmentation reduces investment frequency by 25.6% relative to low fragmentation, while effects attenuate at extreme levels. This deterrence operates independently of regulatory intensity, volume, and topic breadth, isolating coordination complexity as the primary friction. The mechanism is a fixed coordination cost: fragmentation reduces deal count but increases average deal size, indicating a “flight to quality” where firms abandon marginal investments. High-R&D firms uniquely mitigate these costs through absorptive capacity; this adaptation is driven by organizational capabilities rather than financial slack, as cash-rich but low-innovation firms fail to adapt. These patterns emerged post-2010, following the Dodd-Frank expansion of regulatory oversight.

  2. Working Paper
    When Investors Don’t Trust the NAV: Valuation Opacity, Runs, and ‘Retailization’ of Private Equity
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Accepted: UNC Alternative Investments Conference 2026
    Abstract SSRN Link

    I explore how valuation opacity in private equity funds can lead to run-like behavior among retail investors. As private equity "retailizes," I argue that the lack of transparent, market-based NAVs creates a first-mover advantage for redemptions during liquidity crunches.

  3. Working Paper
    Should Founders Raise Funds from VCs?
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Presentations: Babson College Entrepreneurship Research Conference (BCERC) 2026
    Abstract SSRN Link

    Venture capital is widely viewed as a uniform accelerant for startups. We argue that VCs function instead as extreme polarization agents, transforming entrepreneurship from a steady business game into a “lottery ticket” game. Using a sample of U.S. startups, we document a “Hollow Middle”where VC backing provides zero marginal benefit to the bottom 75% of ventures (p > 0.30) while driving a 2-fold valuation surge at the 90th percentile (β90 = 0.71). We identify the causal mechanism as a Governance Shock. Using a dynamic difference-in-differences framework robust to staggered timing, we find that VCs do not merely select high-variance firms but actively engineer volatility. Immediately upon investment (t = 0), the probability of founder departure spikes by 1.3 percentage points (z = 7.53) and accelerates to 5.4 percentage points by year 2 (z = 19.94). This active destabilization forces a binary “up-or-out” trajectory, structurally eliminating moderate outcomes to maximize tail-end returns.

  4. Working Paper
    Worker (Mis)classification Laws and Entrepreneurship
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Presentations: Babson College Entrepreneurship Research Conference (BCERC) 2026
    Abstract SSRN Link

    We examine how worker classification laws reshape entrepreneurial ecosystems by compelling firms to reclassify independent contractors as employees. Exploiting California’s Assembly Bill 5 as a quasi-experiment, we document that high-propensity business applications declined 10.8 percentage points in contractor-intensive sectors. Effects persist and intensify through 2024, revealing regulatory costs operating through deterred venture formation, capital reallocation, reduced employment growth, and organizational restructuring. The evidence shows that classification laws generate substantial spillovers beyond targeted industries, fundamentally altering the cost structure and viability of contractor-dependent business models with lasting implications for entrepreneurial dynamism and innovation capacity.

  5. Working Paper
    What Happens When Startups Grow Too Fast?
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abstract SSRN Link

    This paper analyzes the long-term survival rates of startups that experience "hyper-growth" in their early stages. I document a "speed penalty" where firms that scale premature to product-market fit face a 20% higher likelihood of failure in subsequent rounds.

  6. Working Paper
    Mass Shootings and Angel Investments
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abstract SSRN Link

    I exploit the exogenous shock of local mass shootings to study risk aversion in angel investing. I find that angel investment activity in affected counties drops significantly in the quarter following an event, suggesting that local violence increases perceived tail risk for early-stage investors.

  7. Working Paper
    Economic Policy Uncertainty and VC Exits
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abstract SSRN Link

    I analyze how Economic Policy Uncertainty (EPU) influences Venture Capital exit strategies. Finding that high EPU periods are associated with a significant decrease in IPOs and a corresponding increase in M&A exits, I suggest VCs prioritize liquidity speed over valuation maximization during uncertain times.

    Robo-advisory: The new paradigm in asset management or a millennial fad?
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2025
    Abstract SSRN Link

    This paper evaluates whether robo-advisory services represent a fundamental shift in asset management or a transient trend. Comparing risk-adjusted returns of robo-portfolios against traditional advisory benchmarks, I find evidence supporting the efficiency of automated rebalancing.

    Frank Ellis, Titus Ebenezer Kwofie, Mathias Awuni, and 2 more authors

## **Major Projects**

I am a doctoral researcher in entrepreneurial finance. My research explores the impact of external shocks on entrepreneurship and entrepreneurial finance. I am particularly interested in how these shocks influence the formation and growth of new ventures, their access to financing, and the outcomes of investor exits.

 Ongoing projects:

- **Economic Policy Uncertainty and Investor Behavior**  
  Understanding how venture capital (VC) and angel investors respond to uncertainty, new legal regimes, and digital platforms. I examine exit strategies such as secondary sales and continuation vehicles, and explore how digital media (e.g., micro-influencers) can affect startup fundraising.

- **Regulatory Frictions and Entreprenurship**  
  Studying the economic consequences of worker classification laws on business closures, startup scaling, and employment reallocation. This includes causal identification using difference-in-differences (DiD) and Bartik shift-share IV methods.

## **Minor Projects**

- **Business Angel Syndicates**  
  Co-authored an entry in the *Encyclopedia of Private Equity*, examining how angel syndication shapes information asymmetries, portfolio diversification, and investment dynamics in early-stage finance.

- **Robo-Advisory: The New Paradigm in Asset Management or a Millennial Fad?**  
  A critical study of how algorithm-driven advisory platforms are reshaping asset management, with attention to their appeal among younger investors and implications for incumbent firms.

- **The Impact of AB5 on Entrepreneurial Dynamism**  
  Ongoing empirical work measuring the effects of California’s AB5 law on startup formation, venture capital investment, and nonemployer firm dynamics using panel data from BDS, NES, and VC databases.

- **Micro-Influencers and Startup Fundraising**  
  Developing a novel empirical strategy to test whether social media micro-influencers help attract venture financing for early-stage firms. Uses panel regressions, event studies, and hazard models to trace engagement and capital access patterns.

- **Venture Exits through Secondary Sales**  
  Exploring the rise of secondary sales as a dominant VC exit mechanism, and their implications for firm maturity, ownership dispersion, and valuation signaling.

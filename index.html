## Welcome!

I am a PhD Candidate in Finance at Stevens Institute of Technology.

My research examines how financing, regulation, and market structure shape entrepreneurial outcomes. I focus on three broad areas:

Venture Capital and Startup Dynamics. I study how venture capital financing affects the distribution of startup outcomes, including growth trajectories, exit paths, and failure rates.

Regulation and Entrepreneurship. I investigate how policy and institutional changes, such as worker classification laws and regulatory complexity, alter incentives for founders, investors, and workers, with consequences for firm entry, exit, and risk-taking.

Private Markets and Investor Behavior. I explore how information frictions and valuation opacity in private markets influence investor decisions and capital flows.

Research Highlights

  1. Working Paper
    Regulatory Complexity and Corporate Venture Capital
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abs

    I investigate how increasing regulatory complexity impacts the investment strategies of Corporate Venture Capital (CVC) firms. Using a dataset of CVC deals from 2010-2024, I find that higher regulatory burdens lead CVCs to shift away from early-stage startups toward later-stage, lower-risk investments.

  2. UNC AIC
    When Investors Don’t Trust the NAV: Valuation Opacity, Runs, and ‘Retailization’ of Private Equity
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Presentations: UNC Alternative Investments Conference 2026 (Scheduled)
    Abs PDF

    I explore how valuation opacity in private equity funds can lead to run-like behavior among retail investors. As private equity "retailizes," I argue that the lack of transparent, market-based NAVs creates a first-mover advantage for redemptions during liquidity crunches.

  3. BCERC 2026
    Worker (Mis)classification Laws and Entrepreneurship
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Presentations: Babson College Entrepreneurship Research Conference (BCERC) 2026
    Abs

    I study the causal effect of worker misclassification laws (e.g., AB5) on entrepreneurial entry. Using state-level variations in legal definitions of "employee," I show that stricter classification reduces gig-economy entry but increases the quality of incorporated startups.

  4. Working Paper
    What Happens When Startups Grow Too Fast?
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abs

    This paper analyzes the long-term survival rates of startups that experience "hyper-growth" in their early stages. I document a "speed penalty" where firms that scale premature to product-market fit face a 20% higher likelihood of failure in subsequent rounds.

  5. Working Paper
    Mass Shootings and Angel Investments
    Mathias Awuni
    Working Paper, 2026
    Abs

    I exploit the exogenous shock of local mass shootings to study risk aversion in angel investing. I find that angel investment activity in affected counties drops significantly in the quarter following an event, suggesting that local violence increases perceived tail risk for early-stage investors.

  6. Palgrave
    Business angel syndicates
    Stefano Bonini and Mathias Awuni
    In The Palgrave Encyclopedia of Private Equity, 2023
    Abs HTML

    This encyclopedia entry provides a comprehensive overview of business angel syndicates, detailing their structure, deal-sourcing mechanisms, and their distinct role compared to traditional venture capital funds in the private equity ecosystem.

  7. JBD
    Workforce diversity and team effectiveness: Insights from the construction sector in Ghana
    Frank Ellis, Titus Ebenezer Kwofie, Mathias Awuni, and 2 more authors
    Journal of Business Diversity, 2019
    Presentations: Academy of International Business (AIB) Annual Meeting 2019
    Abs HTML

    We explore the impact of workforce diversity on team effectiveness within the Ghanaian construction sector. Surveying 300 professionals, we find that while demographic diversity improves problem-solving capabilities, it can also increase communication costs if not managed effectively.
